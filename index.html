about me

My statement

Electronics pricing around the world is at an all-time low. With the current availability of these products, people everywhere are becoming connected with the internet. Making electronics affordable and accessible to people is what I do. Refurbishing, recycling, and reselling is what I do. Anything from computers, tablets, headphones, to iPhones. Millions of electronics every year sit in drawers being unused by anyone. Broken. Alone. Fixing and recycling these electronics to other people helps connect everyone together.

A little about me 

I started out my business adventures at the ripe age of 13. In my free time I would buy iPhones to resell. Eventually moving on I bought higher priced broken phones. Teaching myself how to repair them seemed to be the best choice, since I could buy broken one's cheaper and sell them for more.

Eventually I expanded out. I found a passion for computers and their hardware. I built several gaming PC's, all of which I sold to make a better one. This led me to think, "Hey, maybe there is something better worth my time I could be doing...". That is when I stumbled upon my first business adventure.

I worked towards getting my first batch of wholesale from Hong Kong. This was a huge step for me since I had never bought anything from overseas before. I started my eBay adventure at 15. I will admit, it was a disaster at first; it took me near 4 months to sell my first batch of 25 headphones. Continuing on from there I developed a selling strategy and have been recycling, refurbishing, and reselling ever since.

 My Coding skills

My coding experience has ventured back for about two years. Learning python, never by a book, at the age of 14 was a struggle. I had multiple problems with very little help from other in the community. Eventually, learning the syntax and how programming languages act the hard way benefited me. It is much easier to adopt and read other people's code, as well as learn lower-level languages faster.                                              


Along with my coding experience I have a small background in many Linux distributions. I have grown fond of Linux ever since my first encounter of it. Dual-booting Linux on almost every laptop/desktop I have owned has given me the experience that I need to be able to work around the operating system.
